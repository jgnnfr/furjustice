DID YOU KNOW?

  • Research in psychology and criminology shows that animal abusers tend to repeat their crimes as well as commit similar offenses against members of their own species. This phenomenon is known to law-enforcement and humane professionals as “the link”. For everyone’s safety, it’s vital for law-enforcement officials and communities to treat cases of cruelty to animals seriously and to ensure that animal abusers are appropriately prosecuted and sentenced.

  • Children who frequently see other people abusing animals may imitate and harm another animal as well.

Animals suffer daily from abuse and neglect, often unable to escape. Government, animal welfare, and social service organizations must collaborate to ensure animal safety. Parents should treat animals with kindness, allow them to live in their homes, and avoid exposing children to exploitation-ridden zoos or circuses. Children should also set a good example.
